The Vercel Tax

Published May 2026 · Performance Engineering

I saved a startup $14,000/year on Vercel.

Not by switching hosts. By changing 3 config lines they didn't know existed.

Most teams on Vercel are bleeding 40-60% on unnecessary costs. The bill just grows. Quietly. Predictably. They call it scaling. I call it the Vercel Tax.

The Three Leaks

1. Unoptimized Image Configs

Vercel bills for image bandwidth. Most teams serve JPEGs and PNGs when AVIF is 50% smaller and supported natively by Next.js Image. You're paying for bytes you don't need to send.

2. Default Function Regions

Your serverless functions spin up wherever Vercel defaults them. If 90% of your users are in New York and your functions run in San Francisco (or Dublin), you're paying for cross-continent latency and cold starts.

3. SSR When SSG Would Work

Next.js renders the same marketing page 10,000 times per day. It doesn't change. It doesn't need to be dynamic. But someone left getServerSideProps in the codebase, and now you're paying for compute on every single request.

Fix #1: Pin Your Region

One line. 300ms faster cold starts.

// vercel.json { "regions": ["iad1"] }

Replace iad1 with wherever your users are: fra1 for Europe, hkg1 for Asia, sfo1 for US West. Check your analytics. Match it.

Fix #2: Force Static Export

If the page doesn't need real-time data at request time, make it static. Vercel serves static from edge. Free. Fast.

// next.config.js module.exports = { output: 'export' }

Not every page can be static. But most pages can. Audit your routes. If the data changes daily or slower, static + revalidate beats SSR every time.

Fix #3: Enable AVIF

// next.config.js images: { formats: ['image/avif', 'image/webp'], minimumCacheTTL: 60 * 60 * 24 * 30 }

AVIF cuts image size by 40-60% versus JPEG. On a high-traffic site, that's thousands of dollars in bandwidth. The minimumCacheTTL line tells Vercel to cache optimized images for 30 days instead of regenerating them.

Why Vercel Doesn't Warn You

These aren't bugs. They're defaults. Defaults are safe. Safe is expensive.

Vercel's business model is aligned with your usage, not your optimization. The more you use, the more they earn. It's not malicious — it's just not their job to save you money.
